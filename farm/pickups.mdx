During peak harvest seasons, transporting large quantities of tea or coffee to the factory can be overwhelming. Whether your usual transport broke down, or you are harvesting a new, remote route, the Sasini App allows you to summon our factory fleet directly to your location to ensure your produce is collected while it is still fresh.

---

## 1. Creating a Pickup Request

To ensure the logistics team dispatches the correct size of truck to the right location, you must fill out a precise digital request form.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Pickup Form">
    Navigate to the **Produce Pickups** page and tap **Request New Pickup**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fill in the Produce Details">
    You must provide exact details so we know what to expect:
    * **Produce Type:** Select between Tea Leaves, Coffee Cherries, or Macadamia.
    * **Quantity & Units:** Input the estimated weight (e.g., "500" and select "KGs").
    * **Preferred Date:** Select the exact day you need the truck to arrive.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set the Location">
    Use the integrated GPS map to drop a pin exactly where the produce is gathered. This is critical if the produce is not stored at your primary registered address.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## 2. Mandatory Verification & Submission

To prevent fake requests and wasted factory fuel, the app requires visual proof before a truck is dispatched.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload Photographic Proof">
    Tap the **Camera Icon** to take a live photo, or upload an image from your gallery showing the harvested produce ready in bags or baskets. *Note: The submit button will remain disabled until an image is attached.*
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit Request">
    Once all fields are green, tap **Confirm & Request Pickup**.
    1. The app will sound a **Confirmation Beep**.
    2. A success message will appear on your screen.
    3. The system generates a highly tracking code (e.g., `PU-992-TEA`).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Backend Security: Geospatial Routing Engine" icon="server">
    **Under the Hood:** When a pickup coordinate is submitted, the backend pings a geospatial routing API (like Google Maps Distance Matrix). It cross-references the coordinates with active fleet GPS locations, estimating the optimal route and warning the admin console if a requested location is unreachable by heavy trucks.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Backend Security: Media Sanitization & S3 Storage" icon="server">
    **Under the Hood:** The uploaded proof image is not saved directly to the database. The app strips the image of malicious EXIF data, compresses it, and securely uploads it to an Amazon S3 Bucket. The database simply stores a secure URL link to that bucket, keeping the app blazingly fast.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Backend Security: Idempotent Request Generation" icon="server">
    **Under the Hood:** If a farmer has a poor network connection and accidentally taps 'Submit' five times in a row, the backend uses Idempotency Keys to recognize it is the exact same request. It will only generate one Pickup ID, preventing the dispatch of five duplicate trucks.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 3. Tracking & Support

Once requested, tracking the status of your logistics truck is entirely automated.

<Steps>
  <Step title="In-App Notifications">
    Navigate to your **Notifications** page and tap the **Pickups** tab. Here, you will see your active request, the designated Pickup ID, and real-time status updates (e.g., *Truck Dispatched*).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Contacting Support">
    If there is a delay or you need to speak to logistics, open the **Sasini Support** chat. Simply provide your **Pickup ID**. The support agent will immediately query this ID in their admin system to see the exact GPS location of your assigned driver.
  </Step>
</Steps>
